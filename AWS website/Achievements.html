Prepared by the information and studies directorate at the ministry, the report, which can be viewed at www.moh.gov.jo, shows the “workload shouldered” by the ministry, its efforts to improve health services and its overall achievements.

More Achievements

There are 676 health centres spread around the Kingdom, 102 of which are comprehensive, 380 primary and 194 secondary health centres, the spokesperson said, adding that there are 405 dentistry clinics and 464 motherhood and childhood clinics.

The ministry’s spokesperson said its hospitals face “huge pressures”, with a large increase in people checking in at outpatient clinics, whose number reached 2,978,989.

Jordan was ranked one of the best countries in the region in the field of protecting children against dangerous diseases. The national vaccination programme, implemented in 1979, has successfully protected children from polio, tetanus and diphtheria, in addition to measles and tuberculosis, Petra reported.
